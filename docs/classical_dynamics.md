# Classical Dynamics

This module implements the **classical dynamics of the kicked top system**, which serves as the classical counterpart of the quantum kicked top studied in quantum chaos.

The classical kicked top evolves on the **unit sphere**, where the angular momentum vector

$$
\vec{X} = \left(\frac{J_x}{j}, \frac{J_y}{j}, \frac{J_z}{j}\right)
$$

satisfies

$$
|\vec{X}| = 1
$$

The classical dynamics arise from the semiclassical limit of the quantum kicked top model.

---

# Classical Kicked Top Map
The classical map for the kicked top consists of a **rotation followed by a nonlinear torsion**.

The update rule for the angular momentum vector is

$$
\vec{J}_{n+1} = R_y(p)\,T_z(k)\,\vec{J}_n
$$

where

- \(R_y(p)\) represents a rotation about the \(y\)-axis
- \(T_z(k)\) represents a nonlinear twist about the \(z\)-axis
- \(p\) is the rotation angle
- \(k\) is the kick strength

Explicitly, the nonlinear torsion around $x$-axis corresponds to:
$$
X_1 = X
$$

$$
Y_1 = Y \cos(k' X) - Z \sin(k' X)
$$
$$
Z_1 = Z \cos(k' X) + Y \sin(k' X).
$$

The nonlinear torsion around $z$-axis corresponds to:
$$
X_2 = X_1 \cos(k Z_1) - Y_1 \sin(k Z_1)
$$

$$
Y_2 = X_1 \sin(k Z_1) + Y_1 \cos(k Z_1)
$$
$$
Z_2 = Z_1.
$$
This is followed by the rotation around $y$-axis $R_y(p)$ which mixes the components of the angular momentum vector.

# Phase Space

The classical phase space of the kicked top is the **surface of a unit sphere**. 
The dynamics can exhibit

- regular motion
- mixed phase space
- fully chaotic dynamics

depending on the values of **kicking strengths**. 
For small \(k\), the motion is predominantly regular, while for large \(k\), the system becomes strongly chaotic.

---

# Lyapunov Exponents

The module includes utilities for computing **Lyapunov exponents**, which quantify the sensitivity of the system to initial conditions.

The maximal Lyapunov exponent is defined as

$$
\lambda = \lim_{n \to \infty} \frac{1}{n} \ln \left(\frac{|\delta \vec{X}_n|}{|\delta \vec{X}_0|}\right)
$$

where

- $\delta \vec{X}_0$ is an initial infinitesimal displacement between two nearby points in the phase space, and
- $\delta \vec{X}_n$ is the displacement after $n$ iterations of the map.

A positive Lyapunov exponent indicates **chaotic dynamics**. Here, I have computed the **largest Lyapunov exponent** (LLE) instead of computing the full Lyapunov spectrum. See our article *Study of the double kicked top: A classical and quantum perspective*, Phys. Rev. E **112, 014217 (2025)**, to understand the LLE computations.

# Example Usage

A classical trajectory can be generated by iterating the classical map for a given set of parameters.

Example workflow:

```julia
using QuantumKickedTop

# define parameters
p = pi/2
k = 3.0
kp = 0.0

# initial conditions
theta0 = 0.3
phi0 = 1.2

# number of iterations
steps = 1000

R = spherical_to_cartesian(theta, phi)
# evolve trajectory
for _ in 1:steps
    R = map(R, p, k, kp)
    # record each point to get scatter plot
end
```
The resulting trajectory can be used to analyze the structure of the classical phase space. In this package, I have not computed (but provided all functions to compute) the phase space portrait. You can give it a try.

# Scientific Background

The classical kicked top is one of the most widely studied models in the field of **classical chaos** and provides a direct connection between

- classical nonlinear dynamics
- quantum chaos
- entanglement generation in quantum systems.

It is particularly useful for exploring the **quantum–classical correspondence** in chaotic systems.
